Development of a Solid-State Continuous BP Monitor

Active material actuation and pulse wave analysis replacing traditional pneumatic cuffs[cite: 2853, 2856, 2857].

The Objective

To design a continuous non-invasive blood pressure (CNIBP) monitor that eliminates bulky pneumatic pumps. The system utilizes an active-material elastic sleeve and embedded piezo-electric sensors to track pulse wave amplitude in real-time[cite: 2856, 2857, 2858].

1. Analog Front-End (AFE) Design

Piezo-electric transducers generate high-impedance charge signals proportional to arterial displacement[cite: 2862]. To prevent signal loading, I configured an LMP7721 precision amplifier as a charge-to-voltage converter[cite: 2863]. The hardware AC-coupling acts as a tuned bandpass filter with a sharp cutoff below 0.1 Hz, cleanly rejecting static baseline pressure while passing the 1-2 Hz human heartbeat frequencies[cite: 2864, 2865, 2866].

2. Digital Logic & Patient Safety

The system executes a deterministic 100 Hz sampling routine via Timer1 Interrupts, tracking local maxima and minima to calculate real-time pulse amplitude[cite: 2896, 2898].

Hardware Fail-Safe: Given the compressive nature of the active material, I integrated a hardware Watchdog Timer (WDT) with a 500ms timeout[cite: 2925, 2926]. In the event of a firmware exception or sensor failure, the WDT triggers a global hardware reset, immediately dropping the PWM output to 0% to relax the cuff and ensure patient safety[cite: 2927].

3. Mechanical Architecture

The mechanical build relies on a multi-layer architecture. The inner thermoplastic polyurethane (TPU) sleeve features recessed pockets to ensure a flush, zero-gap mechanical coupling with the skin[cite: 2900, 2901, 2902]. Actuation is driven by Shape Memory Alloy (SMA) wires routed along a rigid polymer spine, converting linear contraction into uniform circumferential pressure[cite: 2903, 2904].
