Physics-Informed Discovery of Battery Materials

Accelerating R&D timelines via Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs).

The Challenge

Standard AI models often fail to identify usable battery materials because they produce chemically unstable results that ignore fundamental laws of physics. Traditional trial-and-error discovery timelines are resource-intensive and often require more than a decade to bring a new material to market.

The Methodology

We architected a machine learning framework that embeds domain-specific physical principles directly into the learning process. Unlike "black-box" models, this PINN is constrained by fundamental laws to enhance accuracy and synthesizability.

  1. Physics-Based Features: Curated 29,377 compounds from the Materials Project and engineered 22 distinct descriptors.
  2. Physics-Informed Core: Constrained calculations using Volumetric Energy Density (VED) principles.
  3. Neural Network Prediction: Trained a Feed-Forward Neural Network to predict VED with high fidelity.

The Discovery

The model demonstrated exceptional predictive fidelity, achieving a coefficient of determination (R²) of 0.9989. This framework enabled the high-throughput screening of 500,000 hypothetical compositions in under 15 minutes.
