Neuromorphic Spiking Algorithms

Modeling biological neuron dynamics for power-efficient hardware.

The Concept

Neuromorphic systems maximize hardware efficiency by replacing "always-on" circuits with event-driven architectures that only transmit data when specific energy thresholds are met.

1. Leaky Integrate-and-Fire (LIF)

This simulation models biological voltage accumulation. The system integrates incoming current but "leaks" voltage during inactivity. Upon hitting a threshold, it fires a spike and resets via a refractory period.

2. Spike-Timing-Dependent Plasticity (STDP)

This algorithm drives dynamic learning. Connection weights strengthen when an input spike precedes an output spike, and weaken when reversed, automatically optimizing data pathways.
